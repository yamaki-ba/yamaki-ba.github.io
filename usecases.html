Demand Planning, Demand Forecasting and Demand Management

Supply Chain Analytics

At its core, Supply chain management is all about matching supply and demand. In order to design an efficient supply chain, the first step is to forecast the demand.

We help you answer the three main questions in this demand process.

Demand Planning: What should we do to create product demand (KPIs of the five Ps)?

Forecasting Demand: If we adopt the particular demand creation plan, what do we expect the demand to be? The answer to this question lies in making forecasts with high degree of confidence.

Demand Management: Given this demand forecast, how do we prepare and act when it starts materializing? The answer to this question balances the demand and supply across the organization.

Identifying the Push-Pull Boundary

Supply Chain Analytics

While managing supply chains, managers evaluate the point or boundary where the supply chain changes from being a push to a pull - which is sometimes called the push/pull boundary, or the push/pull point. This is the place where the inventory management switches from one based on a forecast to actual demand.

The decision is critical because for the same operations and same products it involves totally different supply chains.

Segmentation

Supply Chain Analytics

In reality, no firm operates a single supply chain - whether they realize it or not.

Firms need to clearly segment their products, customers, and suppliers so that they can treat them in a better manner, whether it's more efficiently or more effectively.

We help you measure three main dimensions to segment on:

  • the physical characteristics,
  • the demand characteristics, and the
  • supply characteristics.

Segmentation is not a static decision but needs to be dynamic in tandem with the acceleration or decelaration of the product velocity and other mix characterstics due to seasonality or changing trends. Our models help you maintain the optimal balance at all times.
